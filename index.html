Our Story

Welcome to Bella Napoli Pizzeria, where every slice tells a story. Our journey began in the heart of Naples, Italy, the birthplace of pizza, where our founder, Giovanni Russo, first fell in love with the art of pizza-making. Giovanni grew up in a family where food was not just sustenance but a way of life. His grandmother, Nonna Maria, was a renowned cook in their small village, known for her traditional recipes and her wood-fired oven that was always burning, ready to bake the perfect pizza.


In 1955, Giovanni decided to bring his passion for authentic Neapolitan pizza to the world. He traveled across Europe, learning different techniques and gathering inspiration. In 1960, he settled in Copenhagen, Denmark, where he opened the first Bella Napoli Pizzeria. The pizzeria quickly became a local favorite, known for its warm, inviting atmosphere and its commitment to using only the freshest ingredients.

The Early Days

In the early days, Bella Napoli was a small, cozy restaurant with just a few tables and a wood-fired oven imported directly from Naples. Giovanni insisted on making everything from scratch, from the dough to the sauce, using recipes passed down through generations. His dedication to quality and authenticity quickly earned Bella Napoli a loyal following.

Word of mouth spread, and soon people were coming from all over Denmark to taste Giovanni's pizzas. The restaurant expanded, adding more tables and a larger kitchen, but the commitment to quality never wavered. Giovanni's son, Marco, joined the family business in the 1980s, bringing new ideas and a modern twist to the traditional recipes.

Expansion and Innovation

Under Marco's leadership, Bella Napoli continued to grow. The menu expanded to include a variety of pizzas, from the classic Margherita to innovative creations like the Truffle Funghi and the Spicy Calabrese. Marco also introduced a range of antipasti, salads, and desserts, all made with the same commitment to quality and authenticity.

In the 1990s, Bella Napoli opened its second location in Aarhus, bringing the taste of Naples to even more people. The new restaurant was an instant success, and soon there were Bella Napoli pizzerias in cities across Denmark. Despite the expansion, each restaurant maintained the same warm, welcoming atmosphere and dedication to quality that had made the original Bella Napoli a success.
